# Home HVAC & Water Heater Maintenance Schedules

Regular HVAC and water heater maintenance preserves system efficiency, extends equipment life, and prevents costly emergency repairs. A disciplined maintenance cadence catches small issues before they become failures, keeps energy bills in check, and maintains indoor air quality. This article outlines evidence-based schedules and checklists for residential systems.

## HVAC Filter Replacement

Filters are the most frequently maintained component and have the biggest immediate impact on airflow and efficiency.

**Standard 1–4 inch pleated filters:** Replace every 1–3 months
- 1 month: Pets, high dust/pollen, heavy use (summer/winter peaks), allergies in household
- 2 months: One pet, moderate dust
- 3 months: No pets, low dust, light use

**5–6 inch media filters:** Replace every 6–12 months
**10–24 inch electrostatic/electronic:** Clean/replace per manufacturer (typically 6–12 months)

**Signs it's time sooner:** Visible dirt loading, reduced airflow, system running longer cycles, dust buildup on vents.

*Tip:* Mark replacement dates on a calendar or set phone reminders. Keep a 6-month supply on hand.

## Annual HVAC Servicing

Schedule professional tune-ups **twice per year**:
- **Spring (before cooling season):** AC/heat pump cooling check
- **Fall (before heating season):** Furnace/heat pump heating check

**What a tune-up includes (AC/heat pump):**
- Clean evaporator & condenser coils
- Check refrigerant charge & measure superheat/subcooling
- Inspect condenser fan motor & blades
- Clean drain line, check condensate pump
- Check capacitors, contactors, wiring
- Measure voltage/amp draw
- Check thermostat calibration

**What a tune-up includes (furnace/heat pump heating):**
- Inspect heat exchanger for cracks (critical for gas furnaces)
- Check burner assembly, flame sensor, ignition
- Inspect blower motor, belts (if applicable)
- Check gas connections for leaks (gas furnaces)
- Test safety controls, limit switches
- Measure temperature rise, combustion efficiency (gas)
- Check flue/vent system

**Cost/benefit:** $80–$200 per tune-up, often recouped in energy savings + avoiding one emergency repair. Many contractors offer maintenance plans ($150–$300/year) covering 1–2 tune-ups + priority service.

## Duct Cleaning

**Frequency:** Every 3–5 years, or as needed

**When to clean sooner:**
- Visible mold growth in ducts (verify first—true mold is rare)
- Excessive dust/debris blowing from vents
- Recent construction/renovation
- Pest infestation (rodents/insects)
- Clogged filters (replace first, then evaluate)

**What to expect:** $300–$500 for typical home. Ensure contractor uses source-removal (rotary brush + vacuum), not just "blow and go."

**Note:** NADCA (National Air Duct Cleaners Association) recommends inspection annually, cleaning as needed. Don't over-clean—proper filtration + regular filter changes reduce duct loading.

## Water Heater Maintenance

### Tank Water Heaters (40–80 gal)

**Annual maintenance:**
- **Flush sediment:** Drain 2–5 gallons from tank bottom until water runs clear. More frequent (every 6 months) in hard-water areas.
- **Check T&P valve:** Lift test lever, ensure water discharges. Replace every 5 years or if it doesn't reset.
- **Inspect anode rod:** Check every 2–3 years, replace when 50% depleted (typically every 5–7 years). Extends tank life significantly.
- **Check for leaks:** Around fittings, tank seams, base.
- **Verify temperature:** 120°F (49°C) balances comfort, energy, and scald prevention.

**Signs of trouble:** Rusty water, rumbling/kettling sounds (sediment), pilot light issues, water around base, age >10 years (tank) or >12 years (gas).

### Tankless (On-Demand) Water Heaters

**Annual maintenance:**
- **Descale:** Flush with vinegar or citric acid solution (1–2 hours depending on water hardness). Critical in hard-water areas (>7 gpg).
- **Clean inlet filter:** Screen at water inlet.
- **Check venting:** Ensure no blockages.
- **Inspect gas connections:** If gas-fired.

**Frequency adjustment:**
- Soft water (<5 gpg): Every 2–3 years
- Moderate (5–10 gpg): Every 1–2 years
- Hard (>10 gpg): Annually

**Signs of trouble:** Reduced flow, fluctuating temperature, error codes, longer time to hot water.

## System Lifespans & Replacement Planning

**Typical lifespans (with maintenance):**
- Central AC: 12–15 years
- Heat pump: 10–15 years
- Gas furnace: 15–20 years
- Tank water heater: 8–12 years (gas), 10–15 years (electric)
- Tankless water heater: 15–20 years

**When to consider replacement:**
- Age >70% of expected lifespan + major repair needed
- Efficiency gain >20% with new model (calculate ROI)
- Frequent repairs (>$500/year)
- Refrigerant type (R-22 = phased out, expensive)
- Consistent comfort issues despite tuning

## Seasonal Checklist

**Spring (March–April):**
- [ ] Schedule AC tune-up
- [ ] Replace HVAC filter
- [ ] Clean around outdoor condenser (2 ft clearance)
- [ ] Check thermostat batteries
- [ ] Inspect condensate drain

**Summer:**
- [ ] Replace filter if needed (monthly in peak heat)
- [ ] Listen for unusual noises
- [ ] Monitor energy bills for spikes

**Fall (September–October):**
- [ ] Schedule furnace/heat pump tune-up
- [ ] Replace HVAC filter
- [ ] Check carbon monoxide detectors
- [ ] Test heating system early (before cold snap)
- [ ] Inspect vent/flue for blockages

**Winter:**
- [ ] Replace filter if needed
- [ ] Keep vents unblocked
- [ ] Check for drafts around windows/doors (reduces load)

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Skipping annual tune-ups:** Small issues compound; a $150 tune-up prevents $1,500 emergency repairs.
2. **Using wrong filter MERV:** MERV 8–11 is ideal for most homes. MERV 13+ may restrict airflow in older systems.
3. **Oversizing AC/furnace:** Leads to short-cycling, poor humidity control, reduced life.
4. **Ignoring water heater anode rod:** Replacing it every 5 years can double tank life.
5. **Not flushing tank heaters:** Sediment reduces efficiency, causes noise, shortens life.
6. **Waiting for failure:** Proactive replacement at end of lifespan is cheaper than emergency + water damage.
